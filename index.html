Hi, I'm Songyan (Sunny) Yu and my Chinese name is 于松延. I'm a scientific researcher working on rivers and lake as a Research Fellow at Australian Rivers Institute, Griffith University in Australia. My research can be used to inform the management of river catchments and lakes.

My recent research work focuses on climate change impact and adaption. I recently led a research project to model surface water availability across river catchments to inform water security for freshwater biodiversity and human society under climate change. I was also involved in a study to investigate health risks of purified recycled water as a supplementary and climate resilient water source introduced to reservoirs. My lake-related research has been used to inform the Gold Coast City Council of nutrient reduction in a recreational lake and support water utilities for climate change adaption.

I also have a research interest in better understanding the hydro-ecology of non-perennial streams. This interest starts from my PhD, where I investigated the spatio-temporal dynamics and hydro-ecology of non-perennial streams in eastern Australia. The focus was on quantifying spatial and temporal patterns of flow intermittency, and development and application of a predictive model for surface water extent to identify aquatic habitat refuges across river networks.

I like computer programming and have recently published an R software package – “dycdtools” – that can help lake modelers automatically calibrate a widely used 1-D lake model DYRESM-CAEDYM. The package also includes several visualization functions to post-process lake model output for easy interpretation.

If you find anything interesting in my research, you're welcome to reach out to me via email.
